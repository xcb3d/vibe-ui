In the heart of the ancient kingdom, where shadows danced upon the stone walls of the citadel, a conspiracy was brewing. The King, known for his bold proclamations and ruthless taxation, had decided to rebuild the eastern tower in a style that would defy the gods themselves. "Make it brutal!" he shouted to his architects. "Let the stone speak of power, let the edges be sharp enough to cut the sky!"

The people whispered in the marketplaces. They spoke of the cost, of the labor, of the sheer audacity. But the architects, fearing for their heads, drafted plans that were unlike anything seen before. Massive blocks of granite, reinforced with iron beams, rising like a fist towards the heavens.

As the construction began, the noise was deafening. Hammers struck metal, stone ground against stone. The dust rose in great clouds, choking the birds mid-flight. Yet, amidst the chaos, a strange beauty emerged. The rawness of the materials, the unrefined surfaces, the honesty of the structure—it was a new language of design.

Generations later, scholars would debate the King's sanity, but none could deny the impact of his vision. The tower stood as a testament to an era where function met form in a collision of violence and art.

API Reference

The scroll area component is a wrapper around the native scroll functionality, providing custom cross-browser styling.

Prop Type Default Description
asChild boolean false Change the default rendered element for the one passed as a child.
type "auto" | "always" | "scroll" | "hover" "hover" Describes the nature of scrollbar visibility.
scrollHideDelay number 600 If type is "scroll" or "hover", the delay in ms before the scrollbar is hidden.
dir "ltr" | "rtl" - The reading direction of the scroll area.
